The Beauty of Conservation

Sri Lanka, an island located in the Indian Ocean, is known for its diverse animals and stunning natural surroundings. One of the country's noteworthy residents is its thriving turtle population, which has caught the interest and admiration of both locals and visitors. These turtles play a crucial role in maintaining the ecological balance of Sri Lanka's marine ecosystems.Conservation efforts in Sri Lanka have been pivotal in safeguarding the turtle population.  Along the coast, numerous turtle conservation programs and dedicated turtle hatcheries have been built. These programs seek to maintain turtle nesting sites, monitor turtle populations, and increase awareness about the need of turtle conservation.Join us as we discover the beauty and magic of these wonderful creatures that grace the coasts of this amazing island nation.

Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery

The Hikkaduwa Hatchery and Rescue Center is a facility with the mission of protecting some of the world's most endangered species of turtles. It is located a few kilometers north of the main Hikkaduwa town. It is just one of numerous turtle hatcheries along the island's southern coast. Originally created as a community project to help safeguard the southern coast's biodiversity, it is now a fully-fledged hatchery and rescue facility with devoted personnel that are on duty 24 hours a day to study, look after, and safeguard various turtle species.

The hatchery and rescue facility currently cares for five different species of turtles. These species include the Green Turtle, Hawksbill Turtle, Leatherback Turtle, and Olive Ridley Turtle. Modern water tanks are included in the facilities at the sea turtle hatchery and rescue center, which also cares for adult turtle species that have been pulled from the water after suffering injuries.

Threats To Turtles

When looking at threats to turtles, numerous factors, such as habitat loss, climate change, unsustainable fishing methods, pollution, and illegal wildlife trafficking, pose risks to turtle populations. Important nesting grounds and ecosystems are destroyed by human activities including coastal development, deforestation, and pollution. Nesting beaches and hatchling sex ratios are affected by climate change. Bycatch and becoming tangled in fishing gear have an influence on turtles as well. Ingestion and entanglement are two ways that pollution, especially marine debris, can be dangerous. Turtles are the target of illegal wildlife trade for their shells, flesh, and as pets.
