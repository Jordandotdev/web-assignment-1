Bentota Turtle Hatchery

A recognized facility dedicated to preserving and safeguarding the lives of sea turtles, the Bentota Turtle Hatchery is situated on Sri Lanka's southern coast. The hatchery functions as an essential refuge for sea turtles in the area thanks to its scenic setting and immaculate beaches. The hatchery concentrates on saving turtle eggs, releasing hatchlings into the ocean, and rescuing and treating injured turtles. A committed group of volunteers and environmentalists works tirelessly to rescue and care for wounded or stranded turtles at the Bentota Turtle Hatchery. The hatchery provides shelter and specialized care for turtles who have been damaged by boat propellers, caught in fishing nets, or harmed by pollution. The team's goal is to treat and release these turtles into their natural environment through medical support and rehabilitation programs, assuring their survival and helping to further the conservation of the species.

The preservation and maintenance of sea turtle eggs is one of the goals of the Bentota Turtle Hatchery. In order to discover and move fragile turtle nests to safer locations, the hatchery works with the neighborhood residents. The hatchery greatly improves the odds of a successful hatch and the survival of the hatchlings by protecting the eggs from dangers like predation and human interference. Visitors to the hatchery get the chance to observe the hatching process, take part in releasing the young turtles into the ocean, and develop a bond with these amazing animals.

The Bentota Turtle Hatchery is an important site for the direct preservation of sea turtles as well as a tourist destination with educational amenities. The hatchery increases awareness about the difficulties encountered by sea turtles and the significance of their survival through engaging exhibits, enlightening seminars, and escorted tours. The hatchery hopes to motivate visitors to support turtle conservation efforts and make constructive contributions to the maintenance of marine ecosystems by fostering environmental awareness and ethical tourism practices. In general, Sri Lankan Sea turtles have reason to be hopeful thanks to the Bentota Turtle Hatchery.
